As a part of my final project for an Information Systems class, I had the opportunity to design and publish several charts measuring profitability in the United States. With sample data from 2015, I was able to create several not only functional, but easy-to-use, readable, and aesthetic datatables. Feel free to explore them, and keep reading below for a more in-depth analysis.

What I find so interesting about the Tableau Interface, which I used to design these datatables, is that they allow just about any customization one can imagine. With the click of a button, you can change the color scheme, data visualization, graph distribution, method of measurement, or dataset. You can also "zoom in" on your data to really analyze it. For instance, my datatables visualize different depths of data analysis, starting with the most broad: profitability of the United States. I then was able to go in and analyze the most profitable state -California- and even determine which product type was most profitable. My final datatable allowed me to project sales for upcoming months, specifically with that product type. Being able to feel comfortable designing and utilizing this tool has been an incredible asset and can be used in a wide range of professional settings and departments.
